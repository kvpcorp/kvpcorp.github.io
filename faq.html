What is an Air Ambulance?

An air ambulance is a critical care helicopter or helicopter that is specially equipped with an approved flight stretcher, specialized medical equipment and carrying drugs and medication to treat a patient. An air ambulance is staffed with a fully trained medical team to handle the specific needs of a patient who needs to be transported by air.

Who needs an air ambulance?

Air ambulances are especially effective in transporting a patient in need of emergency medical care over long distances in safety, while at the same time providing timely medical care. This life saving service caters to all age-groups and can be used to transport patients to hospitals for emergency medical care. An air ambulance can also be of vital importance when a patient has to be quickly and safely moved from one hospital to another which has more specialized facilities.

To whom is this service available?

Subscribers of 'Aviators Air Ambulance' shall be entitled to the air medical services within the operations areas as provided by Aviators Air Ambulance for the period for which the member has enrolled for. Aviators shall provide air medical services in the form of helicopters/flights to the member and other persons covered under the membership clauses agreed to in the subscription form.

To whom is this service available?

The only cost will be the subscription charges one pays for the fixed term. Subscription ensures that the subscriber will have no out-of-pocket expenses for being flown to the nearest appropriate medical facility deemed as necessary by Aviators (Services).

Where all can I avail this facility?

Presently, in the first phase, our service with three helicopter will be operational in South Indian states of Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh and Tamil Nadu. In the second phase, Western and Northern regions of India will be covered in the next 12 to 18 months, with an addition of 7 to 10 helicopter/ Helicopters.

Which helicopter do use for the Air Ambulance service?

Aviators Air Ambulance is using brand new Airbus Helicopters H135 twin engine helicopters. The H135 is widely used in the Helicopter Emergency Medical Service (HEMS) role and has over 70 percent market share in this space. Our brand new helicopters offer the highest level of safety, reliability and comfort. Our H135's are flown by trained air crew and staffed with qualified medical professionals to tackle medical emergencies.

How much does this life saving service cost?

Incredibly, this life saving service is available to you and your loved ones for less than Rs 50 per day. Click on the subscribe now link on our website to know more.

Does Insurance cover the cost of Subscription?

At present the subscription is not being covered by insurance. However we are working to get the services covered by insurance and are hopeful that we will be there soon.

An aged relative recently had a bad accident and we would like for her to come and live with us now. However, we don't want to travel on road or rail in view of her fragile health. Can you help?

Yes! We can. Our state-of-the-art H135 helicopter will transport your relative closer to you, where you can get obtain the best medical care and also keep a closer watch on their recuperation.

Can you provide International air ambulance evacuations?

Aviators can provide air ambulance transfers to domestic locations and locations abroad provided sufficient notice is provided. Along with our partners we can provide the highest medical care, required by a patient in need.

Can I avail the air ambulance service if I am not a subscriber?

Unfortunately we cannot offer this service to non subscribers at this point of time. This service is exclusively for the people who are part of the Aviators subscriber family.

What if there are no helipads around where I stay or work?

Our helicopters can land on any hard surface which the pilot deems fit. However we have tied up with a leading emergency service provider to support our operation and they will ensure that the patient is attended to and reached to us for further transportation in case of non availability of landing spots.

Can another person from the family accompany the patient in the helicopter?

We have specifically trained a set of Nurses / Doctors on board of the helicopter. The patient will be sufficiently taken care of. Due to the constraints, we would not allow anybody other than the crew on board.
